About Me

Welcome to my personal webpage! I am passionate about artificial intelligence and its applications in various domains.

Dr. Basel Barakat is a Senior Lecturer of Data Science within the School of Computer Science at the University of Sunderland. His scholarly pursuits revolve around a diverse spectrum of research interests, encompassing applied machine learning, time-series forecasting, wireless communication, and the Internet of Things (IoT). Dr. Barakat's scholarly contributions have garnered widespread recognition, evidenced by several esteemed accolades, including the TOP10 2021 MDPI Future Internet High Cited Series paper for his seminal work on 6G, the prestigious Champion of Champions Award bestowed by the Royal Academy of Engineering, and distinction for delivering the best MSc project.

Dr. Barakat obtained his foundational academic degrees from the University of Greenwich, culminating in a Bachelor of Science (BSc), Master of Science (MSc), and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in the years 2013, 2014, and 2019, respectively. Furthermore, he expanded his academic horizons by completing a postgraduate certificate in Learning, Teaching, and Assessment Practice in Higher Education from Edinburgh Napier University in 2022. This notable achievement merited him a Fellowship to the Higher Education Academy (FHEA).

Prior to his current appointment at the University of Sunderland, Dr. Barakat served as a Lecturer within the School of Engineering and Built Environment at Edinburgh Napier University. His earlier professional engagements include a tenure as a research assistant at the University of Greenwich, where he concentrated on the development of machine learning algorithms for non-destructive flaw detection in pipelines. Notably, his doctoral journey was enriched by a visiting scholar appointment at the University of Cambridge. Additionally, in 2014, Dr. Barakat assumed the role of Research Fellow at the University of Greenwich, collaborating with British Telecom (BT) on a project titled "Towards 5G: Air Interface Techniques."

Dr. Barakat's research prowess extends across various domains, notably encompassing data science, time-series forecasting, wireless communication, real-time systems, information freshness, the Internet of Things, mathematical modeling, and the strategic application of machine learning methodologies to address real-world challenges, particularly in the realms of sustainability and healthcare.
